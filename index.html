Decorate your yard with tulips

During the spring months, tulips can add beauty and elegance to any bouquet or garden. The flowers offer a variety of color choices, a simple elegance and the durability to last for days, even after being cut.

A tulip bulb is an amazing container: a swollen underground stem that stores food energy. It contains a miniature plant with flower, leaf, and root parts, all ready to spring forth when conditions are right. Although you can grow tulips from either bulbs or seeds, bulbs produce flowering plants faster. A tulip bulb produces a plant that will usually bloom the following year. Tulip seeds take only a few months to germinate, but it can be several years before the plant bears flowers.
